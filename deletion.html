Privacy Policy

Arrow Escape doesn't ask who you are. It just keeps your puzzle progress where you left it.

Last updated: June 2026
🏹

THE SHORT VERSION Arrow Escape does not require an account and does not collect your name, email, or any way to personally identify you. Your level progress lives on your device first, and is mirrored to an anonymous cloud profile only so you don't lose it. We use Firebase for anonymous sync, analytics, and crash reports, and Google AdMob for optional rewarded video. Nothing is sold.

01

Information We Collect

Arrow Escape does not ask for your name, email address, phone number, or any other information that identifies you personally. There is no sign-up screen, no login, and no profile to fill out.

The only identifier the app ever creates is an anonymous, randomly generated ID issued by Firebase the first time you open the app (see Section 3). It cannot be traced back to you by us.

02

Local Storage on Your Device

Your real progress lives in a local database (Hive) stored entirely on your device. This includes:

  • Level results — stars earned, best time, and score for each completed level.
  • Streak data — your current and longest daily streak, and the date you last played.
  • Hint balance — how many hints you currently have available.
  • Settings — sound, haptics, and your chosen theme.

This local copy is the source of truth the app reads from every time you play — it works fully offline and is never blocked on a network connection.

03

Anonymous Account & Cloud Sync

On first launch, Arrow Escape signs you in anonymously using Firebase Authentication. This creates a random ID with no name, email, or password attached — you are never asked to provide any of those.

A copy of your progress (streak, stars, hint balance, settings) is periodically mirrored to a Firestore document tied to that anonymous ID. This exists purely as a backup — if you ever reinstall the app on the same device, or in a future version that supports restoring progress across devices, this copy lets you pick up where you left off.

This sync runs quietly in the background and fails silently if you're offline — it never interrupts or blocks gameplay.

04

Analytics

We use Firebase Analytics to understand how the app is used in aggregate — for example, which levels players tend to get stuck on, or how often a hint is used. Events logged include things like a level starting or finishing, a hint being used, or a streak milestone being reached.

These events carry gameplay details (level number, difficulty tier, time taken, stars earned) and are tied only to the anonymous installation ID described above — never to your name or any personally identifying detail, because we never collect one.

05

Crash Reporting

Arrow Escape uses Firebase Crashlytics to automatically report crashes and errors. A crash report typically includes the device model, OS version, and the state of the app at the moment it failed — useful for fixing bugs, not for identifying you.

06

Remote Configuration

The app checks in with Firebase Remote Config to read a small set of tuning values — for example, the starting hint balance for new players, or whether optional ad placements are currently enabled. This is a one-way read of generic configuration values; no personal data is sent as part of this request.

07

Advertising

Arrow Escape shows ads in exactly two situations, and only when you choose to trigger them:

  • Tapping "Free Lives" when you've run out of lives on a level, in exchange for a short rewarded video.
  • Tapping a hint when your hint balance is at zero, in exchange for a short rewarded video.

There are no banner ads anywhere in the app, and no ad ever interrupts you mid‑level uninvited. Rewarded ads are served through Google AdMob, which may use device identifiers (such as the advertising ID) to serve and measure ads, governed by Google's Privacy Policy. An optional, infrequent interstitial ad between levels is built into the app but is switched off by default and only enabled later via Remote Config, never by default at install.

08

Permissions We Request

Internet — required for anonymous progress sync, analytics, crash reporting, and rewarded ads. The app is fully playable offline; this permission only affects background sync and ads.

Vibration — used to drive haptic feedback (a tap, a blocked move, a level complete buzz). Can be turned off entirely in Settings.

Arrow Escape does not request camera, microphone, contacts, location, or storage access.

09

Data Retention

Local data on your device persists until you clear it yourself — via in‑app actions, Android's "Clear Data" setting, or uninstalling the app. See our Data Deletion page for exact steps.

The anonymous Firestore backup is retained for as long as the associated installation remains active, and can be deleted on request — again, see the Data Deletion page.

Aggregated analytics data is retained according to Google's standard Firebase Analytics retention settings.

10

Children's Privacy

Arrow Escape is rated for general audiences and contains no mature content. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children, because we never ask for it in the first place. If you believe a child has been improperly exposed to advertising content through the app, please contact us at the email below.
